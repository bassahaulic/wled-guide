WLED Setup
Walkthrough

Everything you need to get your new LED strip and Gledopto WLED controller up and running. Follow along step by step — you'll be glowing in minutes.

☑

What You'll Need

Make sure you have all of these on hand before starting:

  • 🎛️ Gledopto GL-C-016WL-D WLED Controller
  • 💡 WS2811 LED Strip (addressable, 12V)
  • 🔌 12V DC Power Supply rated for your strip's total wattage
  • 📱 Smartphone (iPhone or Android)
  • 📶 WiFi Network (2.4 GHz — WLED does not support 5 GHz)
1

Wire Your LED Strip

The Gledopto controller has two LED outputs. GPIO16 is the default and should be used first. GPIO2 is a secondary output you can enable later in the app.

WireController TerminalDescription
VCC (Red) VCC +12V Power
DATA (Green) DATA (GPIO16) Signal wire to LED strip DIN
GND (Black/White) GND Ground — must be shared between controller & strip
⚠️ Important: Double-check all connections before powering on. Do not connect or disconnect wires while the power supply is on.

💡 The controller also has extended GPIO ports (18, 19, 25, 33) for advanced DIY use — you can ignore these for a basic setup.
2

Power On & Connect to WLED-AP

  1. Power on the controller by connecting your 12V power supply. The first 30 LEDs should light up orange — that means WLED is running.
  2. On your phone, open WiFi Settings and look for a network called WLED-AP
  3. Connect to it using the password wled1234
  4. Your phone should automatically open the WLED welcome page. If it doesn't, open a browser and go to 4.3.2.1
📱

3

Configure Your Home WiFi

  1. On the WLED welcome page, tap "WIFI SETTINGS".
  2. Enter your home WiFi network name (SSID) and password.
  3. Tap "Save & Connect" at the top of the page.
  4. The controller will disconnect from WLED-AP and join your home WiFi. Reconnect your phone to your home WiFi.
  5. The controller now has a local IP address on your network. You can find it in your router's admin page or through the WLED app (next step).
ℹ️ Both the SSID and password are case-sensitive. Make sure they match exactly.

4

Download the WLED App

The WLED app gives you full control of your LEDs from your phone — colors, effects, presets, and more.

💡 Bassahaulic WLED Controller 🍎 iOS — App Store 🤖 Android — GitHub
✨ Try our Bassahaulic WLED Controller — a simplified, installable web app you can add to your home screen. No download needed!

Once installed:

  1. Open the WLED app and tap the "+" button in the upper right corner.
  2. Tap "Discover Lights" — the app will scan your network.
  3. When you see "Found WLED!", tap the checkmark to add it.
  4. Your controller will now appear in the app's device list. Tap it to open the control panel.
💡 Make sure your phone is on the same WiFi network as the controller. Discovery won't work across different networks.

5

Configure Your LED Strip

Now tell WLED what type of LEDs you have and how many.

  1. In the WLED control page, tap "Config" (top-right corner).
  2. Select "LED Preferences".
  3. Under Hardware Setup, set the following:
    SettingValue
    LED TypeWS2811
    Color OrderRGB
    GPIO Pin16 (default)
    Length (LED Count)Your strip's LED count
  4. Tap "Save" at the top of the page. The controller will reboot with your settings.
💡 Not sure how many LEDs you have? WS2811 strips typically come in 30 or 60 LEDs per meter. Check your strip's packaging or count the individual LED chips.

6

Explore Effects & Presets

This is the fun part! WLED comes loaded with 186+ lighting effects right out of the box.

Color Picker — Tap the color wheel to choose any color. You can also set a specific brightness using the slider.

Effects — Scroll through the effects list to try them out. Popular ones include:

  • 🌈 Rainbow — Classic flowing rainbow cycle
  • ✨ Twinkle — Random sparkling LEDs
  • 🔥 Fire — Realistic fire simulation
  • 🏃 Chase — Moving dot patterns
  • 🎵 Audio Reactive — Effects that respond to sound (requires mic setup)

Presets — Found a combo you love? Save it as a preset so you can recall it instantly. WLED supports up to 250 presets.

Segments — Split your strip into separate zones, each running different effects and colors independently. Great for creating custom lighting scenes.

🎤 Bonus: Microphone Setup (Audio-Reactive Effects) ▶

The Gledopto controller has a built-in microphone for sound-reactive lighting effects. Here's how to enable it:

  1. In the WLED control page, tap "Config" → "Usermods".
  2. Find "Digital Mic" and set these values:
    SettingValue
    Microphone TypeGeneric I2S
    I2S SD Pin26
    I2S WS Pin5
    I2S SCK Pin21
  3. Tap "Save", then power cycle the controller (unplug and replug power). The mic only activates after a full reboot.
  4. Back on the control page, tap "Info" at the top, then toggle the button next to "Audio Reactive" to enable it.
  5. Browse effects marked with a ♫ symbol — these respond to sound!
ℹ️ Audio-reactive effects are marked with ♫ in the effects list. Try GEQ (graphic equalizer) or Gravimeter for starters.
⚡ Bonus: Relay Configuration (Power Saving) ▶

The controller has a built-in relay on GPIO 12 that can completely cut output power when LEDs are off, saving energy.

  1. Go to Config → "LED Preferences".
  2. Scroll down to find "Relay GPIO".
  3. Set the GPIO to 12 and make sure "Invert" is unchecked.
  4. Tap "Save" to apply.
⚠️ The relay only cuts power from the controller's output terminals. If your LED strip has additional power injection points, those will remain powered.

Troubleshooting & Reset

Physical Button Functions:

Short Press Turn LEDs on/off
Hold 1 sec Cycle through colors
Hold 10 sec Factory reset — re-enables WLED-AP hotspot

Common Issues:

No LEDs light up Check wiring connections and verify your power supply voltage matches (12V for WS2811).
Can't find WLED-AP Hold the Function button for 10 seconds to factory reset and re-enable the access point.
Wrong colors Check Color Order in LED Preferences. WS2811 should be set to RGB.
App can't find device Make sure your phone and the controller are on the same 2.4 GHz WiFi network.
Only some LEDs work Check your LED count in Config → LED Preferences. Set it to match your actual strip length.

App-Based Factory Reset:

  1. Go to Config → "Security & Updates" (at the bottom).
  2. Scroll down to "Factory reset" and check the box.
  3. Tap "Save". The controller will reset and re-enable the WLED-AP hotspot.
